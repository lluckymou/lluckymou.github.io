Résumé

Download a copy of my Résumé in a printable format.

For more personal information, see About Me.

In a world ever-increasingly defined by quantity over quality, I aspire to create meaningful software that can inspire others and promote the well-being of society. As an aspiring Software Engineer, I understand and promote the idea of prototyping and analysing ideas before they're turned into code, as it leads to better written and performing programs with greater maintainability. For me, rewriting software is never not an option: If something isn't working, I at least consider rewriting it completely, making it more future-proof and expandable (you can only take the same car to the mechanic so many times before it justifies buying a new car).

In the information age, it's easy to come across bad pieces of advice, and developers need to be able to separate what works from what doesn't (not all StackOverflow posts will be able to resolve your problems, especially when your problem is derived from a lack of understanding of how the environment your programing in works). To me, being a developer isn't about being able to program in a specific language, but in a specific paradigm: Coding in ASP.Net won't make one a better Spring Boot programmer, but understanding how the Model-View-Controller design pattern works makes it easier to learn the specific syntax of what will be tackled (that being not only the aforementioned languages but many others), usually taking a couple of days. However, developers usually seem eager to brag about the number of different programming languages they have used in the past, rather than focus on the different types of projects they have worked on before, which is what usually forces them to think outside the box.

It's not uncommon to find developers stuck for several days in completely avoidable situations if the software had been better designed; And this is where the prototyping (Software Engineering) comes in. Time "lost" in prototyping, modelling, and designing the software is often translated into time "gained" in its developing stage, and although hard for some to comprehend, that is exactly my modus operandi (way of doing things).

During the novel coronavirus pandemic, the Brazilian Robotics Olympiad team sought to adapt the sBotics simulator to the context of the competiton.

2018 desc: Este projeto visou o desenvolvimento de uma plataforma de simulação robótica aplicada a um ambiente de robótica educacional, especificamente a simulação dos níveis 1 e 2 da prova prática da Olímpiada Brasileira de Robótica. Na abordagem comum desta prova utiliza-se Kits de Robótica para simular o resgate de uma vítima em um ambiente de desastre. A idéia deste trabalho não é substituir a prova prática mas fornecer uma alternativa para aqueles que desejam testar seus conhecimentos e não possuem kits, permitir o teste de conhecimentos de programação sem danificar o robô, promover a difusão da Robótica Educacional e da Olimpíada Brasileira de Robótica através de um simulador acessível em formato de jogo para smartphones e tablets. O usuário, através do sistema, deverá ser capaz de programar o robô virtual para seguir um conjunto de instruções definidas fazendo uso da linguagem R-Educ e em seguida simular o funcionamento do seu robô em uma arena similar a real gerada aleatoriamente pelo sistema. Como contribuição à comunidade, a ferramenta proposta pode ser usada para aumentar a adesão das escolas brasileiras às metodologias de robótica educacional, ou em competições de robótica.

Technologies used: Unity (UI); C#; HTML + CSS; jQuery (js);

Results: Simulator used as the practical component of the Brazilian Robotics Olympiad for 2020 and 2021, as well as the platform of choice for the RoboCupJunior Worldwide 2021 Rescue Line;

Mineração de dados é uma etapa do processo de aquisição de conhecimento contido em um grande volume de dados. Com a crescente difusão e avanços científicos e tecnológicos tem se tornado cada vez mais complexo estabelecer o estado da arte de determinadas pesquisas e ferramentas. The project description below was written in 2017, and translated into English in 2019.

Competitions regarding robotics have been increasing around Brazil and the world. In the national scale, the Brazilian Robotics Olympiad (BRO, or OBR in Portuguese) became the biggest robotics competition in all of Latin America. From 2016 to 2017 the number of participants increased by 27%, representing an increase of over 40 thousand students. In the State of Rio Grande do Norte, in that same year, the competition had 198 teams with over 700 competitors. This scenario raises some problems, mainly regarding the logistics and infrastructure necessary to provide efficiency to keep the tournament running for only two days. Granting service and support to the maximum quantity of people possible by the minimum amount of staff available. This project aimed to increase the efficiency of the olympiad, by making an extensive data analysis on the current state of the BRO.

The project description below was written in 2016, and translated into English in 2020.

All around the planet, different population groups suffer from the lack of water, mainly driven by water wastage or mismanagement. Because of the global scale of the situation, all sectors of society are being educated to develop different solutions for this critical problem. This imminent reality motivated the development of this platform, with the main objective of creating a water-saving culture among the students of Natal's IFRN Campus Central high school and community college. The app features a simple interface, where every student or professor can take pictures of a water-related problem around the campus and report it to the water management department.

Technologies used: Java; PHP; HTML + CSS; jQuery (js);
